Down South

To the T (32 mi)
A classic route that goes down towards the "T"-shaped intersection between Southon and Elmendorf. Start at the Mila Make Ready Location. Stops available at the Valero or Shell on Southon ~18 miles in. Strava Link
Elmendorf Loop (38 mi)
Like the T, but longer. Does a counter-clockwise loop around the town of Elmendorf. Start at the Mila Make Ready Location. Stops available at the Valero or Shell on Southon ~18 miles in. Strava Link
Southern Century (100 mi)
A century which goes down south before heading east to La Vernia and back into town. Stops available approximately every 30 miles, starting with the Graytown gas station. Please use their restrooms; don't pee outside of the gas station. The next stop is in floresville, then Sutherland Springs, then La Vernia, and then the Four Points stop just outside of St. Hedwig. Strava Link
Sandy Oaks (53 miles)
Starting at the Make Ready Mila, head south on Southton to Elmendorf. Hop on the Anderson Loop briefly before heading to Sandy Oaks and making a return on Blue Wing. Beware of dogs on Blue Wing Road! Stops available at the Shell (Southton/I-37 intersection, mile 18) and Sandy Oaks (~mi. 35). Strava Link
Fake Race (40 miles)
Starting at the Make Ready Mila, head south on Southton to the Shell. Do a loop, watch out for wild dogs, and go all the way back on Presa. A Sunday classic! Strava Link

Questlike

Bad Idea (206.5 mi)
A 200-mile joyride that hits up the very best of Central Texas cycling. Stops available at New Braunfels, the Feed Store in Blanco, Fredericksburg, Boerne, and anywhere inside 1604. The sections on Nacogdoches out to NB, 306 by Canyon lake, and the return on Blanco are a little traffic-heavy, so this route is best done on the weekend and started early before traffic gets too bad. Strava Link
SATX -> ATX (99.6 mi)
A lovely century between San Antonio and Austin. Take the Amtrak on the way back (usually runs from Austin at about 6pm) for an excellent multi-modal transit day. Starting at Mila on Broadway, head north to Canyon Lake and then push onto Austin. If you'd like, you can also go up to New Braunfels before heading west to Canyon Lake. Stops available along the way. The sections on S 1st and S Congress in Austin are definitely more trafficy than is ideal, so stay aware. Strava Link

Out-of-Towners

Gummy Bear (35 mi)
Starting at a gas station on State Hwy 46, enjoy some lumpy roads just north of San Antonio. Bonus climb to Canyon Lake, if you're feeling brave. Stop available in Sattler, about halfway through the ride. Strava Link
Bullis, Blanco, and Bulverde (60 mi)
Courtesy of Spectra Racing, start at Bullis County Park and head out on some hilly roads to Boerne. Enjoy a pastry at Bear Moon Bakery & Cafe, slightly off the route, before heading to Bulverede and back to Bullis. There is a stop available at Luke's County Mart in Bulverde, but it's slightly off the route as well. Strava Link
