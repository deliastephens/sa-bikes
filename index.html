Down South

To the T (32 mi)
A classic route that goes down towards the "T"-shaped intersection between Southon and Elmendorf. Start at the Mila Make Ready Location. Stops available at the Valero or Shell on Southon ~18 miles in.
Elmendorf Loop (38 mi)
Like the T, but longer. Does a counter-clockwise loop around the town of Elmendorf. Start at the Mila Make Ready Location. Stops available at the Valero or Shell on Southon ~18 miles in.
Southern Century (100 mi)
A century which goes down south before heading east to La Vernia and back into town. Stops available approximately every 30 miles, starting with the Graytown gas station. Please use their restrooms; don't pee outside of the gas station. The next stop is in floresville, then Sutherland Springs, then La Vernia, and then the Four Points stop just outside of St. Hedwig.
Sandy Oaks (53 miles)
Starting at the Make Ready Mila, head south on Southton to Elmendorf. Hop on the Anderson Loop briefly before heading to Sandy Oaks and making a return on Blue Wing. Beware of dogs on Blue Wing Road! Stops available at the Shell (Southton/I-37 intersection, mile 18) and Sandy Oaks (~mi. 35).

Questlike

Bad Idea (206.5 mi)
A 200-mile joyride that hits up the very best of Central Texas cycling. Stops available at New Braunfels, the Feed Store in Blanco, Fredericksburg, Boerne, and anywhere inside 1604. The sections on Nacogdoches out to NB, 306 by Canyon lake, and the return on Blanco are a little traffic-heavy, so this route is best done on the weekend and started early before traffic gets too bad.
SATX -> ATX (99.6 mi)
A lovely century between San Antonio and Austin. Starting at Mila on Broadway, head north to Canyon Lake and then push onto Austin. If you'd like, you can also go up to New Braunfels before heading west to Canyon Lake. Stops available along the way. The sections on S 1st and S Congress in Austin are definitely more trafficy than is ideal, so stay aware.
